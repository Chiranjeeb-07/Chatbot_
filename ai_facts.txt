Q: What is the difference between narrow AI and general AI?
A: Narrow AI, or weak AI, is designed for specific tasks—like recognizing faces or translating languages—and excels within those limits. General AI, or strong AI, aims to mimic human intelligence fully, handling any intellectual task a person can. Today, we have narrow AI everywhere (e.g., Siri), but general AI remains a future goal, still theoretical and unachieved.

Q: What is a convolutional neural network?
A: A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) is a type of neural network specialized for processing images. It uses filters to detect features like edges or textures, making it perfect for tasks like identifying objects in photos or spotting tumors in X-rays. CNNs power much of modern computer vision, from Instagram filters to self-driving car cameras.

Q: What is transfer learning?
A: Transfer learning is a machine learning trick where a model trained on one task is reused for another. For example, a neural network trained on millions of general images (like ImageNet) can be fine-tuned to recognize medical scans with less data. It saves time and resources, making AI more accessible for specialized jobs.

Q: What is overfitting in machine learning?
A: Overfitting happens when a machine learning model learns the training data too well—including its noise and quirks—losing the ability to generalize to new data. Imagine memorizing answers for a test but failing a pop quiz. Techniques like regularization or more data help prevent it, ensuring the model stays flexible.

Q: What is a chatbot and how does it work?
A: A chatbot is an AI program that simulates human conversation. It works by processing input (like your question) using Natural Language Processing, then selecting or generating a response based on rules or learned patterns. Q: What is computer vision?
A: Computer vision is an AI field that teaches machines to interpret visual data, like images or videos. It’s how your phone unlocks with your face, or how a drone avoids obstacles. It relies on techniques like neural networks to analyze pixels and recognize patterns, turning sight into understanding.

Q: What is the Turing Test?
A: The Turing Test, proposed by Alan Turing in 1950, checks if an AI can fool a human into thinking it’s human too. A person chats with both a machine and a human; if they can’t tell which is which, the AI passes. It’s a benchmark for intelligence, though modern AI often prioritizes utility over imitation.

Q: What is an AI model?
A: An AI model is a mathematical system trained to perform a task, like predicting weather or translating text. It’s built from algorithms (e.g., neural networks) and shaped by data during training. Once ready, it takes inputs—like a sentence—and outputs results, like a translation, based on what it’s learned.

Q: What is data augmentation?
A: Data augmentation boosts machine learning by artificially expanding a dataset. For example, flipping or rotating images of cats creates “new” training samples without collecting more photos. It helps models learn better and avoid overfitting, especially when real data is scarce.

Q: What is explainable AI?
A: Explainable AI is about making AI decisions clear to humans. Complex models like neural networks can act like “black boxes,” so XAI develops ways to show why an AI denied a loan or flagged an email. It’s crucial for trust, ethics, and debugging in fields like medicine or law.

Q: What is AI?
A: AI, or Artificial Intelligence, refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks include things like learning from experience, solving problems, understanding language, recognizing images, or making decisions. Essentially, AI is like a super smart tool that can mimic some of the ways humans think and act, but without the coffee breaks or existential crises.
